Understanding the Calculations

This calculator compares two scenarios over your chosen time horizon: buying a home with a mortgage versus renting and investing the difference. Below are the exact formulas and logic used.

üè† Buying Scenario Calculations

1. Initial Upfront Costs

Down Payment = Purchase Price √ó (Down Payment % √∑ 100)
Transfer Tax = Purchase Price √ó 2%
Other Costs = Purchase Price √ó (Other Costs % √∑ 100)
Total Upfront = Down Payment + Transfer Tax + Other Costs
Loan Amount = Purchase Price - Down Payment

The upfront costs represent your initial cash outlay when buying. This same amount becomes the renter's initial investment.

2. Monthly Mortgage Payment (EMI)

r = Annual Interest Rate √∑ 12 (monthly rate)
n = Amortization Years √ó 12 (total months)
Monthly Payment = P √ó [r(1+r)‚Åø] √∑ [(1+r)‚Åø - 1]
where P = Loan Amount

This is the standard amortizing loan formula. Each payment includes both interest and principal.

3. Interest & Principal Split (Each Month)

Interest This Month = Current Loan Balance √ó (r √∑ 12)
Principal This Month = Monthly Payment - Interest This Month
New Loan Balance = Current Loan Balance - Principal This Month

Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal. After the amortization period ends, no more mortgage payments are made.

4. Property Value Growth (Each Year)

New Home Value = Current Home Value √ó (1 + Appreciation Rate √∑ 100)

The home value compounds annually at your specified appreciation rate.

5. Annual Recurring Costs

Base Value = Current Home Value or Original Purchase Price
(depending on your "Costs Based On" selection)
Maintenance = Base Value √ó (Maintenance % √∑ 100)
VvE Fees = Base Value √ó (VvE % √∑ 100)
Property Tax (OZB) = Current Home Value √ó (OZB % √∑ 100)

6. Mortgage Interest Tax Deduction

Tax Benefit = Interest Paid This Year √ó (Tax Rate √∑ 100)
‚ö† Only applies during amortization period

Dutch "hypotheekrenteaftrek" allows you to deduct mortgage interest from taxable income.

7. Buyer Net Worth (Equity)

Net Worth = Current Home Value - Remaining Loan Balance

This is your equity in the home - what you'd net if you sold (ignoring selling costs).

üè¢ Renting Scenario Calculations

1. Initial Investment

Starting Investment = Buyer's Total Upfront Costs

The renter starts with the same cash the buyer spent upfront, invested in the market.

2. Annual Rent Increases

New Monthly Rent = Current Monthly Rent √ó (1 + Rent Increase % √∑ 100)
Annual Rent Paid = Monthly Rent √ó 12

Rent compounds annually at your specified increase rate.

3. Annual Cash Flow to Invest

Buyer's Annual Cash Out = Mortgage + OZB + Maintenance + VvE - Tax Deduction
Renter's Annual Cash Out = Annual Rent Paid
Cash Available to Invest = Buyer Cash Out - Renter Cash Out

If the buyer spends more, the renter invests the difference. If negative, the renter withdraws from investments.

4. Investment Growth (Each Year)

1. Add cash flow: Investments += Cash Available to Invest
2. Calculate return: Growth = Investments √ó (Return Rate √∑ 100)
3. Add growth: Investments += Growth

Returns compound annually. The calculation assumes cash flows happen at the start of each year.

5. Box 3 Wealth Tax (Optional)

If "Taxable" is selected AND Investments > ‚Ç¨57,000:
Wealth Tax = (Investments - ‚Ç¨57,000) √ó 1.5%
Investments -= Wealth Tax
If "Tax-Free" is selected: No wealth tax applied

Dutch "vermogensrendementsheffing" (Box 3) taxes investment wealth above ‚Ç¨57,000 at 1.5% annually. This is a simplified calculation.

6. Renter Net Worth

Net Worth = Total Invested Assets

The renter's total investment portfolio value (after returns and taxes).

üìù Important Notes

  • Simplifications: This calculator makes assumptions (e.g., annual compounding, cash flows at year start, no selling costs) to keep calculations understandable.
  • Tax Rates: Tax calculations are simplified. Actual tax situations vary by individual circumstances.
  • Box 3 Threshold: The ‚Ç¨57,000 threshold is approximate and may change with tax law updates.
  • Investment Timing: Investment returns are calculated assuming cash flow differences are invested at the beginning of each year.
  • No Transaction Costs: Selling costs, broker fees, and other transaction costs are not included.
  • Not Financial Advice: This tool is for educational purposes. Consult a financial advisor for personal decisions.
